Token Tots — AI Healthcare Chat Agent

AI Product Engineer · Dec 2025 – Present · Seattle, WA

About this project

Ava is an iMessage-based AI healthcare assistant designed to help university students manage medication instructions and diet guidance after a doctor's visit. Instead of requiring users to navigate a new healthcare portal or unfamiliar app, Ava meets users in a familiar messaging environment and turns messy prescription notes, symptoms, and daily routine information into structured guidance.

The agent supports medication reminders, prescription interpretation, multilingual conversation, and personalized meal planning. Users can send prescription text, photos, or PDFs, and Ava extracts medication details such as dosage, quantity, timing, and food-related instructions. It then schedules reminders around the user’s real routine, including wake time, bedtime, meal times, and skipped meals.

Ava also helps users generate a 7-day meal plan based on their condition, goals, dietary preferences, and medication-related restrictions. The final meal plan is exported as a polished PDF with daily meals and a grocery list, making the output easier to follow than a long text message.

My role

As an AI Product Engineer on TokenTots, I primarily owned Ava's medication reminder system while also designing the overall technical architecture for the agent. My work focused on connecting conversational interaction, structured backend data, medication parsing, reminder scheduling, and user-facing guidance into one coherent iMessage-based experience.

I collaborated closely with another teammate who led the meal-planning feature, while I made sure the medication reminder logic could fit into the broader agent system. I also communicated with teammates working on research, design, and the frontend information page to align the product direction, user needs, and technical implementation.

  • Designed the overall agent architecture, including how iMessage, Supabase, medication processing, reminder scheduling, meal planning, and PDF generation connect together.
  • Built the medication reminder flow that extracts prescription details, asks for the user's daily routine, and schedules reminders around wake time, bedtime, meal times, and skipped meals.
  • Designed reminder logic so alerts include medication context, such as quantity, food timing, timing notes, and relevant dietary restrictions, instead of functioning only as basic clock notifications.
  • Implemented reminder management features that allow users to review, edit, deactivate, reactivate, or check existing medication reminders through conversation.
  • Integrated structured Supabase storage for user routines, uploaded documents, message history, and active medication reminder records.
  • Collaborated with the meal-planning developer to ensure medication-related food restrictions and reminder data could support the broader meal guidance experience.
  • Coordinated with research, design, and frontend teammates to translate user needs, product scope, and technical constraints into a clear capstone experience.
